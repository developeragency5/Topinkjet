Choosing an Inkjet Printer in 2026: A Plain-English Guide

By TopInkjet Team · January 12, 2026 · 5 min read

Inkjet printers have changed more in the last three years than in the previous ten. Refillable Smart Tank designs have demolished cost-per-page expectations, OfficeJet Pro models have closed the gap on text crispness, and ENVY Inspire all-in-ones now produce truly photo-grade output at home. The catch is that all of those choices live under the same word — "inkjet" — and the right pick comes down to one honest question: how much do you actually print?

Pick by Volume First

Under 100 pages a month, an entry-level model like the HP DeskJet 2855e is the right answer. Between 100 and 500 pages a month, the mid-range ENVY and OfficeJet families balance purchase price with reasonable supply cost. Above 500 pages a month, refillable Smart Tank designs like the HP Smart Tank 7301 pay back the higher hardware price in supply savings within months.

Then Pick by What You Print

Photos and crafts? Look at the ENVY Inspire family — every model includes a dedicated photo tray. Long PDFs that you scan and copy regularly? You want a 35-sheet automatic document feeder; a duplex ADF on the OfficeJet Pro 9125e can scan two-sided documents in a single pass. Still need fax? Mobile fax via the HP Smart app routes faxes through your phone — no landline required.

Don't Forget the Room

Office printers belong on a wired Ethernet jack wherever possible — it's faster, more reliable, and survives router reboots. Home printers benefit from quiet operation and a clean design that won't clash with the rest of the room. Match the printer to the space, the volume, and the work — and you'll dodge the regret most printer buyers feel six months in.
